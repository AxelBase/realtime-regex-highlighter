Share Your Regex Instantly with One Click

Published: November 29, 2025 • 6 min read

You finally crafted the perfect regex. Now you want to show your teammate, ask on Stack Overflow, or save it for later.

Most tools force you to copy-paste text + pattern + flags manually.

We made it one-click simple.

How the Magic ?s= Link Works

Every time you type, we:

  • Combine your text, pattern, and flags into a tiny JSON object
  • Compress it using lz-string (up to 90% smaller)
  • Encode it safely for URLs
  • Update the address bar silently with history.replaceState()

Result: a clean, short link like:

.../realtime-regex-highlighter/?s=4Zy8...

Anyone who opens that link gets your exact setup — instantly.

Real-World Use Cases

  • Share a tricky log-parsing pattern with your team
  • Ask for help on forums with full context
  • Bookmark your favorite regex snippets
  • Embed in documentation or tutorials

Privacy First

Everything stays in the URL and browser. No server ever sees your text.

FAQ

How long can the shared text be?

Up to ~4,000 characters reliably (URL length limits). For longer text, use a gist + link.

Can I disable URL sync?

Yes — just don’t type anything. The ?s= param only appears when content exists.
