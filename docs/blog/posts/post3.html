Mastering Regex Flags

Mastering Regex Flags: g, i, m, s, u, y Explained

Published: November 29, 2025 • 8 min read

Regex flags change everything. One checkbox can turn a broken pattern into a perfect match. Let’s see each one in action with live examples you can try right now.

g – Global: Find All Matches

Without g: only first match. With g: every match highlighted + accurate count.

i – Case Insensitive

Matches JavaScript, javascript, JAVASCRIPT with one pattern.

m – Multiline Mode

Makes ^ and $ match the start/end of each line, not just the whole string. Essential for log parsing.

s – DotAll

Lets . match newline characters. Try start.*end with and without s.

u – Unicode Mode

Enables full Unicode support: café, résumé, etc. are matched correctly.

y – Sticky Mode

Forces matching to start exactly at regex.lastIndex. Great for tokenizers and parsers.

FAQ

Can I combine flags?

Yes! Use any combination: gim, u, gy — all supported.

Which flag is most overlooked?

s (dotAll) — most people forget . doesn’t match newlines by default!
